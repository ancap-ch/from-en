## 公共产品理论和安全生产的谬误

1849年，古典自由主义占主流意识形态时，大家普遍认为“经济学家”和“社会主义”是反义词，比利时著名经济学家莫利纳里写道，

> 如果说在政治经济学中有一个公认的真理，那就是：任何情况下，消费者利益最大化的条件是劳动力和交易自由，商品总量才能满足消费者有行或无形的需求量，因为劳动力和交易自由是降价降到最低的充分必要条件。 并且，任何商品的消费者利益始终大于生产者利益。 如今，遵循这些原则，得出了一个严谨的结论：消费者对无形商品利益的追求，使安全生产应遵循完全竞争法则。 由此产生的结果是：任何政府都无权阻止其他政府与之竞争，或需要安保的消费者专门为这种商品而来。[^1]

他对这场争论是这样评论的：要么这个即合乎逻辑又真实，要么经济学所依据的都是不成立。[^2]

对于这个令人不快的（对于所有的社会主义者）结论，显然只有一条出路：由于一些特殊原因，一些特定的商品不适用上述经济推理。 这就是所谓的公共产品理论者下定决心要证明的。[^3] 然而，我将证明事实上特殊商品或特殊原因并不存在，特别是安全生产不会构成任何问题，它与其他服务或商品生产没有任何差异，不管是房屋，奶酪还是保险。 尽管它有很多追随者，但整个公共产品理论都是错误的，华而不实的推理，充斥着内部矛盾，不当的结论，吸引着捉弄着大众的偏见和假设的信仰，但没有任何科学价值。[^4]

那么，为了避免绘制莫里纳利的结论，社会主义经济学家所找到的退路看起来会是什么样的呢？ 自莫里纳利时代以来，对是否存在适用于各种不同的经济分析的商品这一问题，大家通常地回答是肯定的。 事实上，所有现代经济学教科书均强调私人商品与公共物品之间区别的重要性，因为大家普遍承认生产私人商品的资本主义秩序的经济优越性的真理，而普遍否认公共商品的。[^5] 据说一些商品或服务（包括安全）很特殊，因为对它们的享有并不能仅限于那些为其生产确实提供过资金的人。 相反，那些没有提供资金的人也从中受益。 这样的商品被称为公共产品或公共服务（这与私人商品或私人服务相反，这些商品只对那些真正为此付费的人有利）。 由于公共物品这一特殊属性，人们认为至少在没有足够多的数量和足够好的质量的情况下，市场不能生产公共物品。因此，需要政府采取补偿性的行为。[^6]

不同作者所列举所谓的公共物品千差万别。 作者经常对相同的商品或服务分为不同的类别，这使得对一个不可争辩的特殊商品几乎没有分类，这显然预示着所有特质的模糊性质。[^7] 尽管如此, 作为公共物品中特别受欢迎的例子是消防队，他们以免邻居的房子着火，纵然他没有花钱，他仍然从我的消防队中获利；或是警察，尽管邻居没有出资请巡逻队，警察在我家房子周围走动，也吓跑了领居家潜在的窃贼；又或是灯塔，这是经济学家特别珍贵的例子，[^8]灯塔有助于船舶找到路，尽管船舶的主人没有为它的建造或保养贡献一分钱。

在继续对公共物品理论进行介绍和关键性检验之前，我将研究私人物品与公共物品之间的区别的用处，这能有助于决定什么应该由私人生产，什么应该由国家提供或应该得到政府的帮助。 即使是最肤浅的分析也会指出，使用所谓的非排他性的标准会使人陷入深深的麻烦，而不是提出一个明智的解决方案。 反正乍一看似乎有一些政府提供的产品或服务作为公共物品确实达标，但有多少由政府生产的商品和服务能够列入公共物品的行列，确实不清楚。 铁路、邮政服务、电话、街道等诸如此类的都是商品，对它们的使用可以仅限于对它们出资的人，因此它们似乎是私人商品。 就多维度商品“安全”的多方面而言，情况似乎也都是一样的：取出保险金的一切原因，都必须符合私人商品的标准。 但这还不够。 正如许多政府提供的商品看起来是私人商品一样，许多私人生产的商品似乎也符合公共物品这一范畴。 显然，我的邻居可以从我悉心照料的玫瑰花园中获利，他们可以在不帮我打理花园的情况下，享受花园景观。 对于各种各样的改进也是如此，我在我的房产上赚钱获利，也能提高领居房产的价值。 即使那些不把钱投到他人帽子里的人，也能从街头音乐家的表演中获利。 公共汽车上那些没有帮我花钱买除臭剂的乘客，也从我的除臭剂中获利。 每一个遇到我的人都会从我的努力中获益，没有他们的经济支持，我把自己变成了最可爱的人。 如今，所有这些商品—玫瑰花园、物业改善、街头音乐、除臭剂、个人发展—因为他们显然具有公共物品的特征，因此它们就必须由政府提供或政府援助吗？

正如这些私人生产公共产品的例子表明，公共产品不能私人生产，而是需要国家干预这一公共产品理论的论点是一个严重的错误。 显然，它们可以由市场提供。 此外，历史证据告诉我们，所有目前由国家提供的所谓的公共产品在曾经某个时期是由私营企业提供的，甚至在如今也是由一个国家或其他国家提供的。 例如，曾经几乎任何地方的邮政服务都是私人经营的；街道曾由私人出资，有时，现在的街道仍然如此；甚至连令人喜欢的灯塔起初也是私人企业经营的结果；[^9]私人警察，侦探，仲裁员的存在；对病人，穷人，老年人，孤儿和寡妇的援助是私人慈善机构惯有的关注点。 To say, then, that such things cannot be produced by a pure market system is falsified by experience a hundredfold.

除此之外，当以区别公共-私人产品来决定哪些由市场生产，哪些不由市场生产时，就出现了其他麻烦。 例如，如果生产所谓的公共产品对别人只有负面影响，而没有积极影响，或者如果对一些人有利，对其他人不利，那该怎么办？ 如果我的领居他的房子被我的消防队从大火燃烧中保留下来，他却希望（也许是因为他已经超额保险了）他的房子被烧毁；或者，如果我的领居讨厌玫瑰，又或者和我同车的乘客厌恶我的除臭剂的味道呢？ 另外，技术变迁改变了某一既定商品的性质。 比如，随着有线电视的发展，有线电视这一产品（似乎）由以前是公众的已变成私人的了。 物权法的变化（侵占他人财产法）和一个产品由公共-私人产品性质的演变有着同样的效果。 例如，灯塔只在某一范围内是一个公共物品，就像大海是公众（不是私人）所有。 但如果取得一片海洋作为私人财产被许可，这将在纯粹的资本主义社会秩序中存在，灯塔在有限的范围内闪耀，显然，把没有缴费却享受灯塔这一服务的人排除在外成为可能。

离开有点粗略水平的论述，更彻底地调查研究私人产品与公共产品的区别，我们发现它们之间的区别结果是完全不切实际的。 私人产品和公共物品泾渭分明两分法并不存在，这就是为什么在如何区分既定物品存在如此多的分歧。 所有物品或多或少带有私人性或公共性，随着人们的价值观和评价标准的变化，所有物品都会（不断地会）伴随着私人性/公共性程度而变化，也会随着人口结构发生变化。 In order to recognize that they never fall, once and for all, into either one or the other category, one must only recall what makes something a good. 某物要成为商品，它必须被人认为它是稀有的。 Something is not a good as such, that is to say; goods are goods only in the eyes of the beholder. 没有什么会是商品，除非有至少一个人对其进行主观地评价。 但是，当商品永远不是商品时，当物理-化学分析法不能确定某物作为经济商品时，那么，显然就没有作为分类私人物品或是公共物品的固定的客观的标准。 它们永远都不会成为私人或是公共物品。 Their private or public character depends on how few or how many people consider them to be goods, with the degree to which they are private or public changing as these evaluations change and range from one to infinity. 即使看似完全私人的东西，一旦别人开始关注它们，比如我的公寓室内，或者我内衣的颜色，它们就成为了公共物品。[^10] 同时，看似是公共物品，比如我房子的室外或是我工作服的颜色，一旦别人不再关注，它们立马变成完全私人的物品。 Moreover, every good can change its characteristics again and again; it can even turn from a public or private good to a public or private bad or evil and vice versa, depending solely on the changes in this caring or uncaring. 如果是这样的话，那么无论做什么决定都不能依据私人或是公共物品的分类。 In fact, to do so it would become necessary to ask virtually every individual with respect to every single good whether or not he happened to care about it—positively or negatively and perhaps to what extent—in order to determine who might profit from what and who should therefore participate in the good’s financing. (And how could one know if they were telling the truth?) It would also become necessary to monitor all changes in such evaluations continuously, with the result that no definite decision could ever be made regarding the production of anything, and as a consequence of a nonsensical theory all of us would be long dead.[^12]

但是，即使有人忽视所有困扰去肯定它，为了去论证私人-公共物品的区别是正确合理的，这个论证也不能证明它应该是什么。 它既没有令人信服的理由，说明为什么公共物品完全不能被生产（假设公共物品作为商品中一种单独的类别存在），也不能提供理由说明为什么应该由国家生产，而不由私人企业生产。 这就是公共产品理论基本要义，它引入了上述概念上的区别：对生产公共产品不做任何贡献或是不出资的人来说，公共产品的积极效应证明了它们的好处。 显然，它们至少不会在有足够多的数量和足够好的质量的完全竞争市场中被生产出来，因为不是所有人从生产中获利，不是所有人会出资让生产成为可能。 为了生产这些商品（它们明显是受欢迎的，否则就不会被生产），政府必须投入和协助。 基于两点，这种推理完全是谬论，但是，在每本经济学教材中几乎都能找到（诺贝尔奖得主的除外）[^13]。

For one thing, to come to the conclusion that the state has to provide public goods that otherwise would not be produced, one must smuggle a norm into one’s chain of reasoning. 否则，从表述中可知：由于它们具有一些特殊特征，一些物品不能被生产，人们永远得不出的结论是：这些物品*应该*被生产。 But with a norm required to justify their conclusion, the public goods theorists clearly have left the bounds of economics as a positive, *wertfrei* science. 相反，它们已经进入了道德与伦理的范畴，因此人们期望一个用伦理学理论作为认知规律，以便他们合理地做他们正在做的事情，并无可厚非地得出他们的结论。 But it can hardly be stressed enough that nowhere in public goods theory literature can anything that even faintly resembles such a cognitive theory of ethics be found.[^14] Thus it must be stated at the outset, that public goods theorists are misusing whatever prestige they might have as positive economists for pronouncements on matters on which, as their own writings indicate, they have no authority whatsoever. 也许，即使他们偶然发展了一些正确的东西，却没有一种详尽的道德理论来支撑。 It becomes apparent that nothing could be further from the truth as soon as one explicitly formulates the norm that would be needed to arrive at the conclusion that the state has to assist in the provision of public goods. 能得出以上结论的模型是这样的：无论何时一个人以某种方式能证明某一特定产品或服务的生产会对别人有积极影响，但根本不会被生产出来，或者不会以一定量一定质生产，除非某些人为其出资，无论国家是直接或间接地帮助下，这些人被迫分担所必须的财务负担，然后允许对这些人使用带有攻击性的暴力行为。 不需要说明太多就能看出：实施这一规则将会引起混乱，因为这等于是说只要他喜欢这样做，他就可以攻击别人。 此外，正如我在其他地方详细论证了[^15]这个模型不会像一个合理的模型一样有道理。 To argue so, in fact to argue at all, in favor of or against anything, be it a moral, nonmoral, empirical, or logico-analytical position, it must be presupposed that contrary to what the norm actually says, each individual’s integrity as a physically independent decision-making unit is assured. 因为只有每个人都不会受到别人的肉体侵犯，任何事情可以先讨论，然后看任何事情是否可能达成一致或有分歧。 互不侵犯原则是论证和可能达成协议的必要前提，因此可以对一个被先验推理法证明的合理模型进行激烈地辩护。

[^6]: Another frequently used criterion for public goods is that of “nonrivalrous consumption.” Generally, both criteria seem to coincide: When free riders cannot be excluded, nonrivalrous consumption is possible; and when they can be excluded, consumption becomes rivalrous, or so it seems. However, as public goods theorists argue, this coincidence is not perfect. It is, they say, conceivable that while the exclusion of free riders might be possible, their inclusion might not be connected with any additional cost (the marginal cost of admitting free riders is zero, that is), and that the consumption of the good in question by the additionally admitted free rider will not necessarily lead to a subtraction in the consumption of the good available to others. Such a good would be a public good, too. And since exclusion would be practiced on the free market and the good would not become available for nonrivalrous consumption to everyone it otherwise could—even though this would require no additional costs—this, according to statist-socialist logic, would prove a market failure, i.e., a suboptimal level of consumption. Hence the state would have to take over the provision of such goods. (A movie theater, for instance, might be only half full, so it might be “costless” to admit additional viewers free of charge, and their watching the movie also might not affect the paying viewers; hence the movie would qualify as a public good. Since, however, the owner of the theater would be engaging in exclusion, instead of letting free riders enjoy a “costless” performance, movie theaters would be ripe for nationalization.) [^12]: In fact, then, the introduction of the distinction between private and public goods is a relapse into the pre-subjectivist era of economics. From the point of view of subjectivist economics, no good exists that can be categorized objectively as private or public. This is essentially why the second proposed criterion for public goods—permitting nonrivalrous consumption (see note 6 above)—breaks down too. For how could any outside observer determine whether or not the admittance of an additional free rider at no charge would not indeed lead to a subtraction in the consumption of a good to others? Clearly there is no way that he could objectively do so. In fact, it might well be that one’s enjoyment of a movie or of driving on a road would be considerably reduced if more people were allowed in the theater or on the road. Again, to find out whether or not this is the case one would have to ask every individual—and not everyone might agree (what then?). Furthermore, since even a good that allows nonrivalrous consumption is not a free good, as a consequence of admitting additional free riders “crowding” would eventually occur, and hence everyone would have to be asked about the appropriate “margin.” In addition, my consumption may or may not be affected depending on who it is that is admitted free of charge, so I would have to be asked about this, too. And finally, everyone might change his opinion on all of these questions over time. It is thus in the same way impossible to decide whether or not a good is a candidate for state (rather than private) production based on the criterion of nonrivalrous consumption as on that of nonexcludability (see also note 17 below).

The “Unanimity Principle” which Buchanan and Tullock, following Knut Wicksell (*Finanztheoretische Untersuchungen*, Jena: Gustav Fischer, 1896), have repeatedly proposed as a guide for economic policy is also not to be confused with an ethical principle proper. According to this principle only such policy changes should be enacted which can find unanimous consent—and that surely sounds attractive; but then, *mutatis mutandis*, it also determines that the status quo be preserved if there is less than unanimous agreement on any proposal of change—and that sounds far less attractive, because it implies that any given, present state of affairs regarding the allocation of property rights must be legitimate either as a point of departure or as a to-be-continued-state. However, the public choice theorists offer no justification in terms of a normative theory of property rights for this daring claim as would be required. Hence, the unanimity principle is ultimately without ethical foundation. In fact, because it would legitimize any conceivable status quo, the Buchananites’ most favored principle is no less than outrightly absurd as a moral criterion. See on this also Rothbard, *The Ethics of Liberty* chap. 26; idem, “The Myth of Neutral Taxation,” pp. 549f.

Whatever might still be left for the unanimity principle, Buchanan and Tullock, following the lead of Wicksell again, then give away by reducing it in effect to one of “relative” or “quasi” unanimity.
